Landfill Card System — When Council Decisions Aren’t Followed

Council approved a new electronic landfill card system with a clear start date. That date passed. The system wasn’t implemented. And instead of explaining why, staff asked Council to change the decision — without a single question being asked.

The decision Electronic landfill cards by April 1, 2026
What happened The deadline passed with no implementation
The result The decision was changed instead of explained
What actually happened

A clear decision. No follow-through.

Jan 6

Council approves the system

Council adopts a resolution to implement an electronic landfill card system with a start date of April 1, 2026.

Mar 28

Concerns are raised

A letter to Council asks whether the project is on track, why timelines are slipping, and why there has been no formal update.

Apr 1

The deadline passes

The system is not implemented. No formal update is provided to Council or residents.

Apr 7

Staff asks to change the decision

A staff report recommends rescinding the original resolution and delaying implementation to September 30, 2026.

The real issue

Council didn’t ask a single question.

During the April 7 meeting, no member of Council asked why the project missed its deadline, why timelines changed, or why the original resolution wasn’t followed.

A decision was made. It wasn’t delivered. And no one was held accountable.

The questions raised publicly were never addressed. Council relied entirely on the staff report and moved forward without discussion.

What I’d do differently

Accountability starts with Council

A

Require updates

If Council sets a deadline, progress must come back before that deadline passes.

B

Ask questions publicly

Council must challenge and clarify — not just accept reports.

C

Explain decisions

If something changes, residents deserve to know why.
