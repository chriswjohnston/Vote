How to vote

Bring ID. Mark your ballot. Make it count.

Voters must show proof of name and qualifying address before receiving a ballot. Photo identification is not required.

01
Confirm you are eligible

You must be a Canadian citizen, at least 18 years old, and a resident, owner, tenant, or spouse of an owner or tenant in Nipissing Township.

02
Bring proof of name and address

Accepted documents can include a driver’s licence, utility bill, property tax assessment, lease, pay stub with address, or government document.

03
Mark your ballot clearly

A notable and significant mark must be placed inside the circle next to a candidate’s name for the vote to be valid.

04
Vote before polls close

Voting closes at 8:00 PM on Election Day. Preliminary results are posted after the count.

Voter list

Make sure you’re on the list.

Who can vote

You may be eligible even if you don’t live here full-time.

01
Resident elector

You live in Nipissing Township

If Nipissing Township is where you live and you meet the eligibility requirements, you may vote in the municipal election.

02
Non-resident elector

You own or rent property here

If you live in another municipality but own or rent land in Nipissing Township, you may be eligible to vote. A spouse of a non-resident elector may also be eligible.

03
Trailer owners

Seasonal use may qualify

Trailer owners in a campground may be eligible if they are entitled to use the land on Voting Day, or for six weeks or more during the election year.

It is the responsibility of the elector to ensure they are qualified to vote.
