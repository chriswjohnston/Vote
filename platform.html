The Platform

Six practical commitments for a better-run Township.

This platform is built around low-cost improvements that make Nipissing Township easier to understand, easier to engage with, and more accountable to residents. It is about making local government work better in visible, concrete ways.

What ties it all together

Transparency Communication Participation Oversight

These commitments are designed to improve how decisions are recorded, explained, tracked, and communicated — without pretending the Township needs expensive reinvention to get started.

The approach

Built for results, not slogans.

Good governance is not abstract. Residents feel it when information is easy to find, when votes are visible, when participation is straightforward, and when Council is clearly setting direction.

That is why this platform focuses on systems: the things that shape whether the Township is understandable, responsive, and accountable year after year.

What you can expect from this platform

  • Low-cost, realistic improvements
  • Clear public-facing benefits
  • Better visibility into decisions and spending
  • Stronger communication with residents
The goal

Make local government easier to follow.

Residents should not need special knowledge, hours of spare time, or persistent digging just to understand what Council is doing. A better Township starts with a clearer public record and a Council that communicates more openly.

01
Public record

A Complete, Searchable Public Record

Goal: Anyone can easily find, understand, and follow Township decisions — past and present.

What this includes

  • Modernized council meeting pages
  • Standardized YouTube naming conventions
  • AI-generated meeting summaries
  • Full by-law and resolution archive
  • Publishing documents as searchable text, not just PDFs

What exists today

  • Information is fragmented and difficult to navigate
  • Recordings are inconsistently named
  • Minutes are time-consuming to interpret
  • By-laws and resolutions are not easily searchable
  • Most documents are locked in PDFs

What this fixes

  • Creates a single, complete, and searchable public record
Cost: Low — builds on existing documents and systems

Already underway — before election day

  • Built a prototype public record at Nipissing.news. Every council meeting and resolution has a searchable home, linked to the source documents — built from publicly available files before I even hold a seat. When elected, this improves significantly: staff can be directed to publish structured, machine-readable records rather than the PDFs I currently have to scrape and process manually.

02
Communication

Clear, Consistent Communication with Residents

Goal: Residents do not have to go looking for information — it comes to them.

What this includes

  • Subscribable public calendar
  • Consistent email newsletter
  • Structured social media updates

What exists today

  • Residents must manually check for updates
  • Communication is inconsistent
  • No predictable rhythm

What this fixes

  • Creates a reliable, proactive communication system
Cost: Minimal — tools are free or low-cost; effort is in consistency

Already underway — before election day

  • A meeting calendar is live at Nipissing.news. It is a start — but it is not the full vision. Today it covers council meetings, scraped from what is publicly available. What it does not yet do is aggregate events from across the Township: recreation programs, museum events, public consultations, committee meetings, and everything else residents currently have to hunt for across multiple pages and sources. As a councillor, I can push for a single, unified, subscribable calendar so residents get one place to follow everything happening in Nipissing Township.

  • Online form: Get on the agenda. Right now, if a resident wants to depute before Council, the process is unclear — there is no obvious form, no plain-language guide, and no single place to submit a request. I will advocate for a simple, public-facing form that walks residents through the process and submits their request directly.

  • Online form: Submit correspondence to Council. Residents should not need to know the right email address or the right wording to have their voice heard. A structured correspondence form — with a clear confirmation that it was received and will appear in the public record — makes participation accessible to everyone, not just those already familiar with how municipal government works.

03
Accountability

Transparency in Decisions and Accountability

Goal: Residents can clearly see what decisions are made and how each councillor voted.

What this includes

  • Amending the Procedural By-law to require recorded votes on all substantive motions
  • Clear, accessible public record of decisions

What exists today

  • Recorded votes are optional
  • Individual votes are not always visible
  • Accountability depends on process, not policy

What this fixes

  • Creates a consistent, enforceable standard of accountability
Cost: Minimal — by-law amendment only; no new tools or staffing required

Specific policy actions I will bring forward

  • Amend the Procedural By-law to strengthen accountability. Three specific changes: mandatory recorded votes for all decisions so every councillor's position is on the public record; creation of a Committee of the Whole, scheduled as needed, to allow fuller discussion before formal votes; and a requirement for the Chair to call for discussion before any vote is held — eliminating the practice of motions passing without visible deliberation.

  • Formalize oversight of Boards and Committees. Every board and committee would be required to submit a written annual report before the first October Council meeting. The Chair — or a designated delegate — would then appear at the second October meeting to present that report and answer questions from Council directly. Boards and committees make decisions that affect residents. Council needs a structured, recurring way to review that work.

  • Establish annual Council direction to Boards and Committees. At the first Council meeting each December, Council would issue a formal written mandate to each board and committee for the coming year. This creates a clear line of direction — from Council to the bodies it oversees — and gives residents a way to hold both accountable to stated priorities.

04
Financial clarity

Financial Transparency and Clarity

Goal: Residents understand how their money is being spent.

What this includes

  • Budget visualizations
  • Clear presentation of financial data

What exists today

  • Budget documents are technical and difficult to interpret
  • Limited accessibility for non-specialists

What this fixes

  • Makes financial information clear and usable
Cost: Low — uses existing budget data; modest annual effort for visual summaries

Specific policy actions I will bring forward

  • Advocate for a resident-focused budget alongside the official document. The Township's budget is divided into operating, capital, and reserves — a structure that makes sense for accountants and is required by law, but tells residents very little about what their money actually does. I will push for a plain-language companion document published each year that reorganizes spending by what it delivers: roads, recreation, fire services, administration, and so on. The numbers do not change — the accounting stays exactly as required. What changes is whether a resident can open a document and understand, in plain terms, where the Township's money goes and why.

What this includes

What exists today

What this fixes

  • Makes participation simple and accessible
Cost: Low — primarily process and website updates

Procurement

Transparent and Fair Procurement

Goal: Residents and businesses understand how Township contracts are awarded.

What this includes

  • Public procurement policies
  • Clear tendering thresholds and evaluation criteria

What exists today

  • Procurement processes are not clearly published
  • Lack of visibility creates uncertainty

What this fixes

  • Creates a clear, auditable procurement framework
Cost: Minimal — focused on publishing existing policies
What success looks like

A Township residents can actually follow.

This platform is ultimately about trust. Trust grows when decisions are visible, when timelines are tracked, when records are organized, and when residents are treated like participants instead of afterthoughts.

None of this requires a dramatic reinvention of local government. It requires a Council willing to care about process, communication, and accountability — and willing to treat those things as core responsibilities.
