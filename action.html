Area 01

How Council Works

01

Mandatory recorded votes and debate requirement

Every councillor's vote on every decision should be on the public record. And before any vote is called, the Chair must ask if anyone wants to speak. These two changes together mean no more motions passing in silence, and no more unattributed decisions.

By-law — Amendment to Procedural By-law: Recorded Votes and Mandatory Opportunity for Debate

THAT the Council of the Township of Nipissing hereby amends the Procedural By-law to require that all votes taken by Council be recorded votes, with each member's vote entered into the minutes;

AND THAT prior to calling any vote, the Chair shall explicitly provide an opportunity for discussion by asking if any Member wishes to speak to the matter;

AND THAT this amendment shall apply to all Council and Committee of the Whole meetings.

02

Establish a Committee of the Whole by by-law

A Committee of the Whole gives all members of Council a structured space to have real conversations about complex files — budgets, strategic priorities, major capital decisions — before a formal vote is required. Rather than cramming everything into regular meeting agendas, this creates a dedicated forum for deeper work. The by-law establishes the committee's existence and purpose. How it runs is up to Council.

By-law — Establish a Committee of the Whole

THAT a by-law be enacted to establish a Committee of the Whole for the Township of Nipissing, comprised of all Members of Council;

AND THAT the Committee of the Whole shall meet as directed by Council resolution for the purpose of detailed review and discussion of strategic, financial, and operational matters prior to formal decision;

AND THAT the Committee of the Whole shall operate under such rules and procedures as Council may from time to time determine.

03

Schedule a Saturday Committee of the Whole meeting

Regular Council meetings are not designed for deep work. Agenda items move quickly, time is limited, and complex files rarely get the discussion they deserve. A Saturday session creates a full working day for Council to actually dig into strategic priorities — not a quick vote, but a real conversation. The accessibility benefit for residents is secondary. The primary purpose is giving Council the time and space to govern properly.

Resolution — Scheduling of Committee of the Whole Meeting

THAT Council directs staff to schedule a Committee of the Whole meeting on a Saturday to allow for extended discussion of strategic priorities and to improve accessibility for Members of Council and the public.

Area 02

Boards & Committees

04

Amend each board and committee by-law to require annual Council direction

Without direction built into the governing by-law, committees operate on inertia — doing what they always did, without Council ever having said whether that is what is needed. By amending the by-law for each body, the annual mandate becomes a structural requirement, not a courtesy. Council must act. Committees must align. The accountability loop is built in.

By-law — Amendments to Recreation Committee, Museum Board, and Cemetery Committee By-laws

THAT the governing by-laws for the Recreation Committee, Museum Board, and Cemetery Committee each be amended to require Council to adopt an annual mandate for each body at the first regular Council meeting in December;

AND THAT such mandate shall outline priorities, objectives, and expectations for the upcoming year;

AND THAT each Board and Committee shall align its activities and recommendations with the mandate adopted by Council.

05

Mandatory annual reporting from boards and committees

Boards and committees should have to account for their year — in writing, before the first October Council meeting. Then the Chair (or a delegate) should attend the second October meeting to present that report and answer questions. Council should hear from the bodies it oversees at least once a year, on the record, in public.

By-law — Board and Committee Reporting Requirements

THAT all Boards and Committees of the Township be required to submit a written annual report to Council no later than the first regular Council meeting in October;

AND THAT the Chair, or designate, of each Board or Committee shall attend the second regular Council meeting in October to present the report and respond to questions from Council.

Area 03

The Public Record

06

Publish every active by-law online

The Township's by-laws page currently states: "A complete list of By-laws is available at the Municipal Office." In 2025. Some by-laws are not available online at all. Others exist in broken or inaccessible formats. Outdated versions sit alongside current ones with no clear indication of which is in effect. I built nipissing.news to solve this before I had a seat. This resolution directs staff to do what should already exist.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Publish Complete By-law Archive

THAT Council directs staff to compile and publish a complete and up-to-date archive of all active Township by-laws on the municipal website;

AND THAT such archive be maintained on an ongoing basis to ensure public accessibility.

07

Standardize Council meeting video naming and archiving

The Township livestreams Council meetings to YouTube. The recordings are inconsistently named, making them difficult to find after the fact. A standardized naming convention — date, meeting type — costs nothing and makes the public record searchable. It is a small change with an outsized impact on transparency.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Standardize Meeting Video Naming and Archiving

THAT Council directs staff to implement a standardized naming convention for all Council meeting livestreams and archived videos, including the meeting date and type;

AND THAT all recordings be consistently published and maintained for public access.

08

Implement a subscribable municipal calendar

Right now, a resident who wants to know when the next Council meeting is, when the Recreation Committee meets, or when a public consultation is scheduled has to check multiple pages across the Township website — if they know those pages exist. A single subscribable calendar fixes this. Residents sign up once and get everything.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Implement Subscribable Municipal Calendar

THAT Council directs staff to implement a publicly accessible, subscribable calendar that includes all Council meetings, Board and Committee meetings, and municipal events.

Area 04

Communication & Access

09

Electronic property tax billing

Digital property tax bills were cited as a key modernization benefit of the original TownSuite software purchase. That feature was never delivered — and when the Township switched to MuniSoft, the module was not purchased. Council was unaware. This motion directs staff to report on what it would actually take to deliver what was promised years ago.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Report on Electronic Property Tax Billing

THAT Council directs staff to prepare a report on the implementation of electronic delivery of property tax bills, including costs, timelines, and system requirements.

10

Municipal email newsletter system

Residents should be able to opt in to receive updates directly from the Township — not have to check the website, follow a Facebook page, or happen to hear something at the landfill. An email newsletter is one of the most basic and cost-effective communication tools available. This motion gets the report on the table.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Report on Municipal Email Newsletter System

THAT Council directs staff to prepare a report on establishing an opt-in email newsletter system for residents, including subscription management and communication guidelines.

11

New municipal website

The current Township website is difficult to navigate, inconsistently maintained, and not designed around how residents actually look for information. A new website should be built around residents first — with Council approving the wireframe before development begins, so the direction is set by elected officials, not just by whoever builds it.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Prepare New Website Wireframe and Implementation Plan

THAT Council directs staff to prepare and present a proposed wireframe and implementation plan for a new municipal website designed to improve accessibility, usability, and public communication.

12

Online forms for public participation and correspondence

If a resident wants to depute before Council or submit correspondence for an agenda, the process is not obvious. There is no form, no plain-language guide, no clear path. Two simple online forms — one to request to present, one to submit correspondence — remove that barrier entirely. This should have existed years ago.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Implement Public Participation and Correspondence Forms

THAT Council directs staff to implement online forms to:

a) Allow members of the public to request to present at Council meetings;

b) Submit written correspondence for inclusion in Council agendas.

Area 05

Procurement

13

Review the municipal procurement process

The Township has run two significant procurement processes in recent years that produced results different from what the process promised — the software replacement and the CAO recruitment. Both are documented. The procurement process needs to be examined, not assumed to be working. This motion puts that review on the agenda.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Review and Report on Procurement Process

THAT Council directs staff to review the current municipal procurement process and report back with findings and recommendations for improvement.

Area 06

Facilities & Assets

The 2025 Asset Management Plan puts a $4,000,000 replacement of the Township Office and Public Works Garage on the horizon by 2035 — less than ten years away. The existing facility "does not meet Township requirements for suitability and safe operations." That is not a small decision. It is a community-defining one. These motions are about getting the information Council needs to make that decision — and others like it — before the deadline makes the choice for us. Good planning is not expensive. Last-minute planning is.

14

Community Centre and Museum review — with a $4M decision looming

Before the Township can make good decisions about its facilities, it needs to know what it has. Usage levels, operating costs, capital needs, and market value if sold. These are basic facts that should be in front of Council before any major decision is made about either building — and with a $4M replacement on the horizon by 2035, major decisions are coming. This motion gets that information on the table.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Report on Community Centre and Museum

THAT Council directs staff to prepare a report on the Community Centre and Museum, including:

— Usage levels

— Operating and capital costs

— Estimated market value of the properties.

15

Recreation asset usage study

The hockey rink and playgrounds are among the most visible public assets the Township maintains. Before spending money improving them — or deciding not to — Council should understand how they are actually used and what improvements would cost. Good decisions start with good information.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Report on Recreation Asset Usage

THAT Council directs staff to prepare a report on the usage of recreational assets, including the hockey rink and playgrounds, and to provide cost estimates for potential improvements.

16

Community Hub feasibility at Heritage Park

If the Township is going to spend $4M on a new office and public works facility by 2035 — and it will need to, according to its own Asset Management Plan — then that decision should not happen in isolation. The Community Centre is aging. The Museum needs attention. The rink is underserved. Fire, offices, storage — everything is scattered. The question worth asking before committing to any single replacement is whether a consolidated Community Hub at Heritage Park makes more long-term sense than rebuilding separate aging buildings one by one. This is also exactly the kind of big-picture question a real Strategic Plan should be answering. This motion starts that conversation with a real proposal, not a half-baked idea from a committee meeting.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Develop Community Hub Proposal for Heritage Park

THAT Council directs staff to develop a proposal for a consolidated Community Hub at Heritage Park, including:

— Integration of municipal facilities

— Recreational infrastructure enhancements

— Preliminary site plan and phased cost estimates.

17

Recreation Coordinator feasibility study

Recreation programming in Nipissing has long relied on volunteers and a committee without professional coordination. The question is whether a part-time or full-time Recreation Coordinator could generate enough in programming revenue, grant funding, and facility usage to offset the cost of the role. This motion gets that analysis done before any hiring decision is made.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Report on Recreation Coordinator Feasibility

THAT Council directs staff to prepare a report on the feasibility of hiring a Recreation Coordinator or Event Planner, including potential costs and opportunities for revenue generation.

Area 07

Strategic Direction

18

Engage a firm to develop a long-term Strategic Plan

The current plan expires in 2026 — an election year. There is no successor in sight, and no public discussion of what comes next. I flagged this in writing in 2023. The Township needs a proper strategic planning process: resident research first, a qualified firm to facilitate, and a Council-approved plan that looks further than the next election. This is the motion that starts it.

Resolution — Direct Staff to Engage Firm for Strategic Plan Development

THAT Council directs staff to engage a qualified firm to:

a) Conduct resident research and consultation;

b) Develop a long-term Strategic Plan for Council's consideration.
